Image from NOAA MANILA, Philippines - The number of areas under Signal No. 1 rose to two dozen on Thursday morning, September 13, as Typhoon Ompong (Mangkhut) slowed down over the Philippine Sea. In a bulletin issued 11 am on Thursday, the Philippine Atmospheric, Geophysical, and Astronomical Services Administration (PAGASA) said Ompong is already 725 kilometers east of Virac, Catanduanes, moving west at a slower 20 kilometers per hour (km/h) from the previous 30 km/h. The typhoon continues to have maximum winds of 205 km/h and gustiness of up to 255 km/h. Signal No. 1 is now raised in: * Batanes * Cagayan including Babuyan Group of Islands * Apayao * Abra * Kalinga * Mountain Province * Ifugao * Isabela * Benguet * Quirino * Aurora * Nueva Vizcaya * Nueva Ecija * Bulacan * Rizal * Laguna * Quezon including Polillo Island * Camarines Norte * Camarines Sur * Catanduanes * Albay * Sorsogon * Burias and Ticao islands * Northern Samar Those areas under Signal No. 1 are in the regions of Cagayan Valley, the Cordillera Administrative Region (CAR), Central Luzon, Calabarzon, Bicol, and Eastern Visayas. Occasional rains with gusty winds are expected within the next 36 hours. Image from PAGASA The typhoon is also enhancing the southwest monsoon or hanging habagat. The enhanced southwest monsoon could trigger moderate to heavy rain in the following areas: * Friday - Palawan, Zamboanga Peninsula, Visayas, Metro Manila * Saturday - Palawan, Zamboanga Peninsula, Western Visayas, Metro Manila Residents of areas affected by the southwest monsoon should be on alert for flash floods and landslides, too. Local government units (https://www.rappler.com/move-ph/211847-dilg-alert-levels-local-government-units-typhoon-ompong-september-2018), the Armed Forces of the Philippines (https://www.rappler.com/nation/211737-afp-response-typhoon-mangkhut-september-2018), the Philippine National Police (https://www.rappler.com/nation/211803-philippine-national-police-luzon-full-alert-typhoon-mangkhut-ompong), and the Philippine Coast Guard (https://www.rappler.com/nation/211722-philippine-coast-guard-statement-typhoon-mangkhut-september-11-2018) were placed on alert to respond to the typhoon. Ompong is the Philippines' 15th tropical cyclone for 2018. The country usually gets an average of 20 tropical cyclones per year.
