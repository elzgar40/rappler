OVER 3,500 STRANDED AT SEAPORTS DUE TO TYPHOON OMPONG IMAGE: https://assets.rappler.com/612F469A6EA84F6BAE882D2B94A4B421/img/E17D25468145451FA90B8A0A79FFA632/coastguard-suspended-july-2-2017_E17D25468145451FA90B8A0A79FFA632.jpg MANILA, Philippines - More than 3,500 passengers were stranded on Thursday, September 13, after sea trips were canceled due to Typhoon Ompong (Mangkhut) (https://www.rappler.com/nation/special-coverage/weather-alert/211923-typhoon-ompong-pagasa-forecast-september-13-2018-5pm). The Philippine Coast Guard (PCG) said at least 3,562 passengers, 378 rolling cargoes, 46 vessels, and 25 motor boats were stranded in various ports as of 4 pm on Thursday. The passengers were at ports in Pasig City, Camarines Sur, Albay, Sorsogon, Catanduanes, Southern Leyte, Northern Samar, Negros Oriental, Batangas, and Quezon. VIDEO: https://www.facebook.com/plugins/post.php?href=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.facebook.com%2Fphilcoastguardofficialpage%2Fposts%2F231464727522189&width=500 At 5 pm on Thursday, the Philippine Atmospheric, Geophysical, and Astronomical Services Administration advised fishermen and others with small sea vessels not to venture out into the seaboards of areas under Signal No. 1, the northern seaboard of Northern Luzon, and the eastern seaboards of the Visayas and Mindanao. The PCG earlier ordered its units in Northern Luzon and Central Luzon (https://www.rappler.com/nation/211722-philippine-coast-guard-statement-typhoon-mangkhut-september-11-2018) to prepare for Ompong. PCG Commandant Admiral Elson Hermogino said they must "remind and prevent all types of interisland vessels from sailing" in areas where tropical cyclone warning signals would be raised. - RAPPLER.COM
